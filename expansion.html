Expansion Bus Types

This lesson covers expansion bus types. Expansion cards expand a computer's functionality or increase its performance. Expansion cards install into the expansion slot on a motherboard. Expansion bus standards define communication specifications as well as physical characteristics of expansion cards and slots.

Common Expansion Bus Types

The following table describes the characteristics of the most common expansion bus types.

Bus Type
Characteristics
Peripheral Component Interconnect (PCI)
PCI was developed to replace the obsolete ISA and VESA bus standards. PCI is processor independent, supports plug-and-play, and is commonly used by devices such as sound cards, network cards, and storage device controllers. PCI runs at 33 MHz (133 MBps) or 66 MHz (266 MBps).
PCI Express (PCIe)
PCIe was developed to replace PCI, PCI-X, and AGP. PCIe uses a serial, full-duplex transmission method with several connection types, defined by the number of transmission lanes (x1, x4, x8, x16, x32). Data rates vary depending on the protocol version and lanes used.
Legacy Buses
Buses that have been replaced by newer types are considered legacy. Examples include AGP, Audio/modem riser (AMR), and Communications network riser (CNR).

Video Cards

Video cards process graphical information for output to an external display. The quality and speed have increased dramatically with PC gaming and cryptocurrency mining. A device called a capture card is growing in use to support streaming and recording games and videos.

Integrated vs. Dedicated Video Cards

You can implement video cards as a dedicated expansion board or integrated with other components.

Integrated Video Cards:
  • Integrate the GPU with another hardware component (e.g., the CPU or motherboard).
  • Share system memory for graphic processing.
  • Are less expensive but less powerful.
  • Are found in most pre-built PCs and laptops.
Dedicated Video Cards:
  • Installed in an expansion slot on the motherboard.
  • Have a dedicated GPU and memory bank.
  • More powerful but more expensive.
  • Used in cryptocurrency mining.

Characteristics of Video Cards

When selecting a video card, keep in mind the following characteristics:

Characteristic
Description
Display Connectors
Video cards have connectors for attaching an external display (DisplayPort, HDMI, USB-C, etc.).
Display Quality
Resolution and refresh rate determine the quality of images and animation.
Processing Capabilities
The GPU handles video rendering tasks, improving efficiency over the CPU.
Memory
Dedicated video cards use high-speed memory (DDR3, GDDR5, GDDR6, etc.) to store graphical data.
Bus Type
Video cards must be compatible with expansion slots on the motherboard (e.g., PCIe x16).

Multi-GPUs

You can link video cards together in a multi-GPU configuration to share the graphic processing load. This is supported by technologies like NVIDIA SLI and AMD CrossFire.

Capture Cards

A capture card is used to capture on-screen content for livestreams or video files. It can connect to game consoles, computers, or cameras.

Connection Types

  • USB
  • PCIe
  • USB-C
  • Thunderbolt

Items Needed

  • Primary device (game console, computer, or camera)
  • Capture card with appropriate connection port
  • HDMI cable
  • High-definition TV or monitor
  • Encoding computer
  • Capture card software
